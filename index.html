Causes of Global Warming

Source: Our World In Data

Ever since the Industrial Revolution, energy usage has grown exponentially. Traditionally, cheap biomass fuels such as timber and peat were burned to create raw energy; however, higher-grade fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas have since replaced them as they are more efficient.

While coal, oil, and natural gas are great for producing energy, they aren't great for our environment. These three sources of energy, known as fossil fuels, are notorious for releasing huge amounts of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. Our forests are unable to pull carbon dioxide out of the air as fast enough as they are being added.

As a result of carbon dioxide accumulating into the atmosphere, more infrared radiation is reflected back to the Earth, causing huge temperature increases. As the climate increases, a phenomenon known as desertification is increased, causing water loss, which may lead to species loss and unintentional forest fires.

If everyone worked harder to become more sustainable, the negative side effects of global warming could be reversed. Using energy more efficiently and only when we need to would reduce the usage of fossil fuels, lessening the amount of greenhouse gases produced that reflect solar radiation back to Earth. We could stop fires, habitat loss, and extinction of species if we took action.

Local Solutions

Source: Photovoltaic Cell

The best solutions include switching to renewable energy sources such as solar photovoltaic cells (solar energy), ground-source heat pumps (geothermal energy), or tidal energy (water power); however, some of these solutions are too expensive for the common person to switch to.

Depending on your household, here are the most common strategies for increasing sustainability:

  Less Lighting

  • This may seem obvious, but people still accidentally leave their lights on during the night.

  Less Heating

  • Natural gas is the most common fossil fuel for heating. Heating is extremely expensive (in terms of electricity), and provides only a temporary effect (as heat is lost in buildings very quickly).
  • So the next time you turn the thermostat up, think about if you really need it. Maybe try putting on a jacket or sweater, or even taking a quick warm shower instead.

  Less Meat

  • Meat requires on average 16 times more energy to produce compared to crops. Reducing your intake of crops will reduce the consumption of energy intensive foods.

  More Public Transportation

  • Public transportation allows many people to ride on a single vehicle, meaning less gas is used per person. Compared to driving alone, this reduces greenhouse gas production by magnitudes.
  • Use buses, trains, carpools, etc.

  More Green Structures

  • Building green roofs can increase albedo (reflection of sunlight), while also providing shade and reducing overall air temperatures.
  • Building rain gardens can help collect rain water, preventing any excess runoff into storm drains and also improve the quality of life.

  More Recycling / Composting

  • Recycling glass bottles, paper, or some forms of plastic allow them to be reused rather than end up in landfills. This allows you to reduce the overall waste output of your household.
  • Composting allows you to reduce food scraps or other compostable materials from ending in the landfill, where they will take longer to decompose. You can use the composted material for garden soil, fertilizer, etc.

Overall, here are some of the simple solutions you can implement to increase sustainability. However, globally, we must work to seek more long-term solutions to address the damages we have already caused.

Global Solutions

This is not a joke. You know what the Joint Chiefs told us the greatest threat facing America was? Global warming.
Source: Remarks by President Biden to U.S. Air Force Personnel and Families Stationed at Royal Air Force Mildenhall

Long term solutions include countries switching to more renewable sources of energy. While renewable sources of energy have their own pros and cons, they don't deplete fossil fuel reserves and don't release greenhouse gases.

The three most used renewable sources of energy today include:

  • Solar Power
  • Hydro Power
  • Wind Power

Benefits and Cons of Solar, Hydro, and Wind Power

Solar Power

No mining of fossil fuels for electricity production Solar Panel farms can displace habitats
Not always active (intermittency)

Hydro Power

  Benefit   Con
No greenhouse gas emissions when producing electricity Reservoir floods behind dam
Reservoir and dam can be tourist attractions Fossil fuel combustion during dam construction
Jobs are created to maintain the dam Reservoir flood displaces human homes
No air pollutants released during electricity generation Algae grows in still-water producing methane
Control of downstream seasonal flooding Very costly

Wind Power

  Benefit   Con
Produces no air pollutants May kill birds or bats
Takes up little space Not always active (intermittency)
Can be installed at sea for higher energy yields Can be an eye-sore or noise distraction for humans
Can be used with farmlands to optimize land usage

Other solutions pushing laws for higher fuel efficiencies (CAFE standards for miles per gallon), subsidizing tax credits electric vehicles, charging stations, and hybrids, as well as switching to nuclear power (as it doesn't release as much greenhouse gases as burning fossil fuels does).
